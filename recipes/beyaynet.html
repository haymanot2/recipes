beyaynet

Ethiopian dishes typically consist of injera, a spongy sourdough flatbread made of fermented teff flour, spread flat over a communal pan (a large platter) and topped with several kinds of spicy and non spicy wats (stews) and vegetables. As I’m vegetarian, my neighbor suggested we make yetsom beyaynetu, the vegetarian combination platter. It’s made up of a mix of vegan curries and vegetables served on a plate of injera. Yetsom means fasting and beyaynetu means combination platter. In principle, the combination platter is very similar in composition, but not flavor, to an Indian thali.
